Addition

Addition is the term used to describe adding two or more numbers together.

The plus sign ‘+‘ is used to denote an addition: 2 + 2.

The + can be used multiple times as required: 2 + 2 + 2.

For longer lists of numbers it is usually easier to write the numbers in a column and preform the calculation at the bottom.

It doesn’t matter in which order you add a group of numbers together as the answer will always be the same:

1 + 2 + 3 + 4 gives the same answer (10) as 4 + 2 + 1 + 3

Adding 0 to any number makes no difference 2 + 0 = 2.

Once you can count to ten you can usually quickly perform additions up to ten.


For example, if you're given two piles of cards, one pile containing 4 cards and the other containing 3 cards, you can count all the cards and come up with the answer: 7.


Subtraction

Subtraction is the term used to describe taking away one or more numbers from another.

Subtraction is also commonly used to find the difference between two numbers.

Subtraction is the opposite to addition.

The minus sign ‘-‘ is used to denote a subtraction calculation, 4 - 2 = 2.

The '-' sign can be used multiple times as required, 8 - 2 - 2 = 4, for example; however, although this calculation is correct it is often simpler to add together multiple numbers for subtraction.

8 - 2 - 2 can be simplified to 8 - 4 (the two 2s were added together to give 4 which is then subtracted from the 8). 8 - 2 - 2 and 8 - 4 both equal 4.

When subtracting, unlike addition, the order of numbers does matter – the first number is the number you are subtracting from, subsequent numbers can be in any order.


10-5 is different to 5-10


10-5 = 5 whereas 5-10 = -5


When numbers of equal value are subtracted from each other the result is always 0. 19-19 = 0

When subtracting 0 from any number the number remains unchanged. 19-0 = 19


Simple subtraction can be easily learned in the same way as addition, by counting:


If Phoebe has 9 sweets and Luke has 5 sweets what is the difference?

Starting with the smaller number (5) and count up to the larger number (9).

6 (1), 7 (2), 8 (3), 9 (4).

Phoebe has 4 more sweets than Luke, the difference in sweets is 4.

So: 9 - 5 = 4.


Multiplication

Multiplication is the repeated addition of a number.

When you ‘multiply’ or ‘times’ a number you add it to itself a number of times, for example 3 multiplied by 3 is the same as saying 3 + 3 + 3 = 9.

Multiplication however is a quicker way of adding the same number many times 3 × 3 = 9.

This calculation is the same as saying, if I have 3 lots of 3 widgets, how many widgets do I have in total?


Basic Rules of Multiplication:

Any number multiplied by 0 is 0. 200 × 0 = 0

Any number multiplied by 1 stays the same. 200 × 1 = 200.

When a whole number is multiplied by 10 we can simply add a 0 to the end (there is one zero in 10). 200 × 10 = 2000.

When multiplying by 100 we add two zeros to the end, by a thousand we add three zeros to the end and so on. 4 × 2000 for example is 4 × 2 = 8 plus 3 zeros: 8000.


Division

Division is the opposite of multiplication in mathematics.

Division allows us to divide or 'share' numbers to find an answer.

Multiplication gives us a quick way of doing multiple additions and division gives us a quick way of doing multiple subtractions.

Division is often considered the hardest of the four main arithmetic functions, remember all division calculations can easily be carried out on a calculator.

When you think about division think about sharing a number equally by the number of times the sum says. For example 10 sweets divided by 2 children = 5 sweets each.

When you divide 0 by another number the answer is always 0. For example: 0 ÷ 4 = 0.

When you divide a number by 0 you are not dividing at all. 7 ÷ 0 is not possible.

When you divide by 1 the answer is the same as the number you were dividing. 5 ÷ 1 = 5.

Any number divided by the same number is 1. 26 ÷ 26 = 1.

Numbers must be divided in the correct order. 10 ÷ 2 = 5 whereas 2 ÷ 10 = 0.2.

Ten pizzas divided by two children is very different to 2 pizzas divided by 10 children.


If Paul has 9 boxes of pizzas and eats 3 boxes a day how many days are there before it runs out?


We can work this problem out by doing a series of subtractions, or by counting backwards in steps of 3.


On day 1 Paul starts with 9 boxes and ends with 6 boxes. 9 - 3 = 6

On day 2 Paul starts with 6 boxes and ends with 3 boxes. 6 - 3 = 3

On day 3 Paul starts with 3 boxes and ends with 0 boxes. 3 - 3 = 0


Paul runs out of fuel on day 3.

A quicker way of preforming this sum would be to divide 9 by 3. 9 ÷ 3 = 3. That is, how many times does 3 go into 9.
