Sentence

A sentence is a collection of words that convey sense or meaning and is formed according to the logic of grammar.

Clear, short sentences are preferable, and more effective, than long, complex ones.


Parts of a Sentence

The simplest sentence consists only of a noun, a naming word, and a verb or action word.


For example, in the sentence “Peter jumped”, Peter is the noun and jumped is the verb.


Pronouns

Peter is the proper noun in the sentence above but can be replaced in following sentences by the pronoun he.

A pronoun is a word that can be used instead of a noun when a noun has already been mentioned.

Other pronouns are she, we, they and it.


Subject and Object Noun

Most sentences have a subject noun and an object noun.


For example, consider the sentence “Blake ran towards the court”.

Blake is the subject noun (a person or thing performing the action of running),

and the court is the object noun (a person or thing towards which the action is directed).

Clear, short sentences are preferable, and more effective, than long, complex ones.


Adjectives

An adjective describes a noun and usually comes before it.

When two adjectives are used to describe one noun, they are set a part with the use of a comma.


For example:

The quick athlete sprinted to the finish line.

The quick, tall athlete sprinted to the finish line.


Adverbs

Adverbs describe verbs. The adverb should always follow the verb.


For example:

Stephen finished his homeworks quickly.

Victor fixed the jigsaw puzzle carefully.


Tense

Verbs, or action words, are expressed in tenses; past, present or future.

The tense of a verb is its setting in time.


For example:

Russel jogged (past tense)

Russel jogs (present tense)

Russel will jog (future tense)


Incorrect Use of Tense

There are also other tenses not covered here.

An important point is to be consistent in your use of tense.

Choose whether you are telling an event in the past, present or future and then be consistent in the use of that tense until there is a good reason for changing.

The incorrect use of tenses is one of the most common mistakes of grammar.


For example, consider the following sentence:

“Julian went to the field and play frisbee”.

The verb went is in the past tense but play is in the present tense.


The correct tensing of the sentence should be:

“Julian went to the field and played frisbee”.
